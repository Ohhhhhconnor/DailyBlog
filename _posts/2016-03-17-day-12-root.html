That's a good question, considering that if I couldn't answer it, my priorities would be unfounded.

I have a lot of debt. About $150k. It makes you feel stuck. It stunts your growth. I need to accelerate my payments and get it out of my life. That's why I would like to supplement my income.

I'm getting older, and getting weaker. I also am aware of the positive links between being fit and how healthy your brain is. That's why I would like to get fit.

I have not been living my life intentionally with my current relationships. I constantly am wishing I would do more with my friends and family that matters. And really, they are the ones in my life who matter. That's why I'd like to deepen those relationships.

I have been slacking at work. I talked a little bit about why in my last post, but that's really no excuse. It's not who I want to be. That's why I'd like to work harder at work.

I have not been reading enough. Faith comes by hearing and hearing comes by the Word of God. By not reading my Bible, I am not contributing to my faith, and therefore I cannot expect all that God has promised will take place in my lifetime. Knowledge is power, and by reading I can acquire knowledge. That's why I would like to read more.

I have hardly any control over where my time is being spent. My priorities are out of whack, and I waste days away like nobody's business. That really needs to end. That's why I'd like to get better at time management.

It's pretty easy for me to point out the reasons behind the things that I want to achieve in the near future, and I think that is a good thing. 

Saying something and doing it are two completely different things. I want to be a man of my word, and do what I say I will do. 

I want to look back at my life and have as few regrets as possible. I want to reap what I have sown and see the fruit of my labor. I want to be the hardest working person I know, all while being the most invested in his relationships.

It's going to take a while for me to get there, but I will. You have my word.
