I watched an amazing TED talk about procrastination today. You can watch it here.

He talks a lot about why procrastinators are the way they are, but it was his point at the end of the video that struck me the most.

You see, procrastination isn't so bad when there is a deadline. I would wait until the last minute to write a lab report or study for a test. It always worked out okay.

The big problem for procrastinators is when they want to get something done that has no deadline. There is no sense of urgency or panic that kicks in.

That's why it is so important for me to learn how to plan. If I can't plan for things that have no deadline, then I will never even start them.

It's simple but it gets fuzzy for me sometimes

I have had some success with working out for my marathon because I have both a deadline and a plan that I am using. It makes my part easy - just do what it says. 

Things like supplementing my income, building relationships, reading more and doing better at work have no deadline and no plan. How do I even remotely expect to succeed without a written plan?

I need dates, I need steps and then I need to take action. As long as I have the other things in place, I don't think that it is too hard for me to take action.

I will be developing specific plans in the future. These plans will need to be tested and adjusted, but they will help me get over the hump of never achieving what I want to because I procrastinate so long.

I need to be the one to initiate and start planning these activities. No one is going to do them for me, and no one is going to care if I don't succeed, because I will still be just average default Jordan.

In the next coming days expect written plans. If I don't deliver, someone please hold me accountable.

Jordan.
