Reiterate My Focus

Keep it simple.


In my first post, I made the statement that "My default self needs to wake up every morning and create, not wake up and consume." I need to revisit this.

Consuming content is almost like a disease. It's the opposite of making an impact on the world - rather, it is making an impact on you.

The content we consume was originally created by someone who took the time and energy to create it. Whether it be art, social posts, information, news, etc., someone made it.

My simplistic goal is to create more, do more and be more. The same thing could be said in this way: My simplistic goal is to consume less, relax less and be less comfortable with who I am.

I really think that I don't need to consume any content. No social media, no tech videos, no vloggers, no news articles. It's just not necessary at the rate we are accustomed to.

My focus is to eventually become the content that people are consuming. My focus is to make an impact on the world, not just let it make an impact on me.

This simple switch will easily give me more time, more energy and more focus on the things that I want to achieve. How can it not?

I'm ready for a big switch in my daily routine, and I think this is a big step.

I'm not banning all content from my life, I'm just going to change the flow direction from consume to create. Part of changing that flow might be stopping the flow all together initially. A reset.

I'm at a unique place in my life where I am sill able to mold my future. There are a lot of things set in stone, but that just helps me focus even more because I don't have to think about accomplishing those things.

I want to mold my future into one of productivity, creativity and enjoyment. A future that I can plan in advance, while being bold enough to have absolutely no idea what will happen next.

Work harder. Do more.

This feels good.

Jordan
