--- layout: post title: 'Day 35: Flexibility' date: '2016-04-14T05:47:00.003-04:00' author: Jordan O'Connor tags: modified_time: '2016-04-14T05:47:46.170-04:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-1202201332823762457.post-736439016217273981 blogger_orig_url: http://dailyblog.jordanmoconnor.com/2016/04/day-35-flexibility.html --- I find it interesting when I look at the motivations that I have behind wanting to start my own business. In the short term, I want to be out of debt. I want to be able to support my family and begin building wealth. But I think this drive goes much deeper than that. It more fits my personality, something that is flexible but stable, something that I created myself. Something I can strive for and push towards everyday. In my mind, I’d like to be able to work wherever I am in the world. I don’t want to take vacations, but rather trips that still allow me to be productive. Sitting on a beach gets old for me really quick. To me, trips are more about experiences than relaxation. I can relax just fine at home. I want to travel for the sake of having unforgettable experiences and see things I never would have seen had I been relaxing. But I also want that flexibility in my everyday life. I want to be able to exercise, see my kids and talk with my wife in the mornings. I want to live close to work, so that I can make sure the commute isn’t an excuse for not seeing them often. I want to work hard, though, and that will require some late nights for seasons and it will require a lot of my attention. That’s part of the reason why I need to waste none of my extra time. I need to spend any extra time on the things that are most important to me. I don’t want ‘work’ to define me, but I want to be defined as a hard worker. I want to be the hardest working person I know. Both at work, and in the home. I want my kids to see that they need to work hard to achieve their dreams. Not only that, I want to teach them that it’s a good thing to have dreams and that they should feel like anything is possible. I want to teach them to learn for themselves, to love other people and build meaningful relationships. I want to teach them that they have control over the path that their life takes, and that it’s not dependent on anything in their past. I want to have the flexibility to play that role in my kid’s lives. Jordan
