--- layout: post title: 'Day 17: It''s a Boy' date: '2016-03-22T23:19:00.001-04:00' author: Jordan O'Connor tags: modified_time: '2016-03-22T23:19:14.138-04:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-1202201332823762457.post-4141227990352666215 blogger_orig_url: http://dailyblog.jordanmoconnor.com/2016/03/day-17-its-boy.html --- I'm having a baby boy, folks!

Today my wife and I had the 20 week ultrasounds to check the vitals of our baby and determine the gender. Let's just say it was quite obvious.

It's crazy to think that I will be fathering a son soon. I can't wait. By that time I will be more productive than ever, because I will need to be.

I will need to be sacrificially ready and available for my family, so I can't afford to slack off. I want to be running my future company by that time, and eventually I would like to get to the point where I can spend time with them and work as well.

To me, I think working is a very important aspect of life. I think that my kids should be a part of that journey. I think they should know how working hard is good for them, and that they too should work hard for the life they want to live.

I want to father my kids under the impression that they truly can be anything they want to be in the world. I want to show them that they can make their own path, and that the people they surround themselves with will impact them the most.

But I also want my kids to be creating and making, not consuming constantly. I want them to get their hands dirty, use their brains, and be inspired to grow everyday.

I want to have an awesome legacy to pass on to my kids, so that they can carry the torch and change the world.

I want to change the world.
