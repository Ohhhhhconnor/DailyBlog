Estimating

To my demise


I used to pride myself in the ability to estimate just about anything, even without a lot of prior knowledge in a subject. I can gauge a lot by how a question is asked, phrased and emphasized.

Growing up I applied this to a lot of things. It helps make you appear to learn faster than everyone else, and sound way smarter than you actually are. 

Precision was never really something that interested me. If I could get close enough faster than anyone else, that was good enough. It worked for a while.

What I didn't realize is that my ability to estimate was diminishing my ability to be precise and follow a plan of action to a specific result.

To this day I can't divide two numbers on paper. I do it all in my head - and if the scope is reasonable, it works great. Until the scope is unreasonable.

I can't estimate what my life is going to be like in 10 years. I can hardly estimate what my life will be like after one year. This lack of specificity has made it so not only can I not follow a life plan, I can't create one either.

I'm 'good' at budgeting our expenses because I can estimate quick numbers in my head, but I don't have a specific plan to actually get a desired result. I just hope it works out based on what I estimated.

This attitude/default tendency in my life needs to not be used so much. It's keeping me from reaching specific results that I want to achieve. I come up with a goal and then I just hope it happens. 

My life needs to be intentional and specific. Not a product of hoping things work out like I thought they would.

This concept may be simple for some people, and that's fine. But it is nearly impossible for me. I am always easy on myself, I'm always prone to make the rules bend in my favor. 

Specific planning needs to be a default tendency of mine. I need to be intentional about my actions and decisions. I also need to be intentional about my priorities. It's crucial for me to stop guessing about my future.

It's time I acknowledged that even because I can get close, that's not the real thing. Close to a great life is not a great life. Close to creating my own wealth is not creating my own wealth. Close to working hard is not working hard. 

I need to figure out how to plan, and be able to follow a plan to the T.

This feels good.
