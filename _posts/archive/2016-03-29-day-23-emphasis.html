--- layout: post title: 'Day 23: Emphasis' date: '2016-03-29T07:15:00.002-04:00' author: Jordan O'Connor tags: - Blog - Vision - Living Intentionally - Daily Blog - Intentional - Help - Leadership - Self Help modified_time: '2016-03-29T07:19:20.524-04:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-1202201332823762457.post-2373601171121575020 blogger_orig_url: http://dailyblog.jordanmoconnor.com/2016/03/day-23-emphasis.html --- I put a lot of emphasis on my progress outside of work, and that's okay. But it's not okay if I'm not doing well at work.

I spend a lot of time thinking about how I will make more money outside of work, how to improve myself outside of work, and my work has suffered because of it.

I'm still stuck in my current situation - somewhat isolated from my old group of engineers. I really want to get back to working with them and get out of the current group I am working in.

I also haven't been looking into how I can make more money at work. Honestly, I know that if I was the hardest worker in the building, they probably would pay me more. I could ask for a raise at a moment's notice.

Is that how I should be approaching this? Should I continue to invest in ways to make more money outside of work, when there is a potential that I could be killing it right where I am at?

I had these questions yesterday when I was sitting at my desk. I mean, a consistent job truly is a great thing. It's up to me to make the most of it. I really should be emphasizing my focus on it since my job is the reason I can put food on the table everyday.

My biggest problem is, I hate how much time it is going to take me to pay off my debt and save for a home and start a business. We're talking like 10 years minimum. That's a long time!

I feel like I can't settle into a routine unless I am making headway towards accelerating my debt payoff. I feel like it's something that I need to do.

I'm pretty sick of making a good salary, yet living paycheck to paycheck. There has to be some ways that I can make more for my family so that we can get this debt paid off sooner rather than later.

Doing something other than what comes naturally to you is really hard. This whole freelancing thing isn't natural to me. Engineering is natural to me.

I think I will talk to my engineering manager about all of this. He seems to be very understanding. I don't bug him much, but maybe I should. I'd like to get his input about how i can make some changes at work.

I'm not a bad worker, but I'm in a bad spot. Something isn't jiving with me. I need to be moving forward both at work and outside of work.

Keep moving forward.

Jordan
